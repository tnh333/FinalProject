More on the housing market in the US

Example West Coast Home

Western style homes can be explained by the influence of a few things. First the gold rush odes to the high class style in certain areas like california. Secondly they have the influence of the people who owned the land before hand drawing inspiration from the traditional Spanish looks and the Latin American culture. Climate also plays a large role in their design. The homes on this side are constructed to withstand earthquakes and sometimes mudslides.

Example East Coast Home

Eastern style homes structural influence comes from the British and European cultures that the people who moved over first were ruled by. They style is traditional Victorian that and you will commonly see brick and stone buildings with ivy covering them. The East Coast's climate also is an influence to how the houses are constructed and the style that they have. The climate on this side is known for heavy storms with snow and hurricanes.

More Info

In this section we will go over some of the deeper analysis of the housing market in the United States. The maps created give us a sense of what is happening, however we can learn more if theses maps are paired with other forms of information as well.

Many people might think that COVID-19 had a negative effect on the housing market but the opposite is actually more true. The corona virus pandemic was no doubt a devestating event that had many negative impacts. However, one area that seemed get better, or at least stay steady was the housing market in the United States. After doing a bit of research we found that there were many factors that went into the reason why the housing market didnt crash. There were a series of "large transfer payments" which include stimulus checks, and unemployment benefits. This meant that income and the demand for housing did not crash. Also, during this time mortgage interest rates were very low which increased the demand for housing. One of the smaller factors is that people were working form home so they could work far from the work place and get homes outside the area.
Source: dallasfed.org

Diving further into some of the reasons why the prices of homes in the US rose during the pandemic we found that rent and supplies for homes also played a large role. The US census gathered some information from Zillow and found that many renters could not afford rent in cities but could afford to buy homes further away since mortgage rates were low. Also Zillow predicted that 2020 would have been a "big home-buying year" due to millennials reaching the age that the average person buys a home.
Sources: census.gov

Another factor that contributed greatly to the increase in the housing market during covid was the supply issues. The prices of housing material greatly rose during the pandemic which in turn raises the prices of homes. Lumber and some of the main metals used to construct houses were at all time highs during this time and that caused the construction of the homes to cost more as well as raising the list price.
